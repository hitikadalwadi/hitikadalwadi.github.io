A quick introduction

I'm a graduate researcher at Northeastern, building AI systems that are accurate, auditable, and genuinely useful.

Currently pursuing an MS in Artificial Intelligence at Khoury College of Computer Sciences, graduating December 2027. Before Northeastern, I spent two-plus years at ZS Associates as a Data Solutions Engineer, building ETL pipelines across 80+ healthcare data sources and working with Salesforce Data Cloud on identity resolution and CRM unification.

My current focus sits at the intersection of retrieval-augmented generation, agent governance, and algorithmic fairness. I won the Most Innovative Use of MCP at the Red Hat × IBM hackathon for Sentinel, an AI agent governance system, and placed second at a Google DeepMind × Cactus hackathon for a hybrid local/cloud inference routing system.

I also write on Substack about ML concepts and what I'm learning, and I care a lot about making technical work legible to people outside the field.
